Squeaky Bikes

Visiting the bike store to have your squeaky bike checked can be frustrating and expensive.

A bike usually squeaks because the moving part are not lubricated enough. However, you may be surprised to hear that parts which are screwed together firmly can also squeak.

  • Lubricating the Seatpost
    The seatpost must be sufficiently lubricated when you mount it in the frame to avoid sticking and squeaking.
  • Lubricating the Steering Stem
    The steering stem of a traditional steering column (threaded headset) must be sufficiently lubricated when you mount it, to avoid sticking and squeaking.
  • Adjusting rim brake toe-in
    Brakes can be noisy and squeak if the distance to the rim hasn't been set correctly.
